Orbital Risk Authority

Quantifying responsibility in space.

The Orbital Risk Authority (ORA) is an independent initiative focused on measuring orbital risk, debris accountability, and space safety for governments, insurers, and satellite operators.

Our core metric, the Orbital Risk Index (ORI), provides a structured way to evaluate how crowded, fragile, and responsibly managed key orbital regions are.

ORA exists to help governments, insurers, and satellite operators navigate an increasingly crowded and strategically critical orbital environment through structured, explainable, and responsible risk assessment.

What ORA focuses on

Global Orbital Risk

High-level scoring for LEO, MEO, GEO and other orbital regimes, combining debris density, collision probability, and long-term sustainability into a single interpretable view.

Accountability & Behavior

Operator-level assessments that consider design choices, maneuver capability, end-of-life planning, and historical behavior.

Policy & Markets

Tools and metrics that can support emerging regulatory frameworks, insurance pricing, and responsible orbital use policies.
